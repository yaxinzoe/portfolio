The website I planned to design can automatically determine the health of the body based on the information entered by each person, such as working hours, eating conditions, mood fluctuations and other conditions. The site assistant can also remind the user of the rest or what to eat every day, providing useful recipes and lifestyle according to the individual's condition. Part B

About

Personal Contribution

Our website combines the history of World War I and the portraits of soldiers. At the beginning I proposed the concept of an archives room or an exhibition hall, putting the portrait of the soldier in the picture frame and hanging it in the background of the exhibition hall in the website. After discussing with my classmates, I changed my original idea. Using an auto-rotating mode to showcase the soldier's portrait frame, the dynamic effect is more cool. In the first attemp, my template was similar to the first one. After showing the effect to the team members, I received feedback from everyone and made changes on this basis. We think that the effect of the first type is that the two cards are at an angle of 90 degrees to the plane, and the portrait of the soldier cannot be displayed well. So I designed the second rotation effect template. For drop-down menus, our web game is an immersive role-playing game. The mechanics of the game are that you can see the current user's personal information by clicking on the soldier's avatar.

Part B

About

Personal Contribution

For presentation and slide, I am mainly responsible for describing the change of web content and functionality from partA to partB. After the first show, some students gave feedback to our webpage theme. They believes that combining the portraits of soldiers with the history of World War I is a good idea, but the scope of World War I history is too broad. It would be better to change the soldiers of World War I to the ANZAC Corps to narrow the scope. After accepting this suggestion, I found the historical background of ANZAC through the Wekipedia and the Australian Legion Memorial, and participated in the famous battles of World War I. In addition to the changes in the theme of the webpage, I have also organized other changes on our website. First, due to the lack of technology, our web page has removed the ranking system. Second, a hat icon is used in the lower left corner of the web page to allow the user to click to exit the game, enhancing the interactivity of the web page. Third, I mentioned earlier that our soldier portrait is automatically rotated, but in the subsequent user test, several users have reported to me that if they can click on them to rotate the portrait box, it can increase the playability of the webpage. At the same time, they can better understand the portraits of soldiers in that era.

Part C

About

Personal Contribution

In the drop-down menu section of partC, through the Australian soldier rank system website(https://www.army.gov.au), I replaced the upper part of the head with the rank, and the ranks continued to increase as the level increased. At the same time, the user level is also upgraded as the number of questions is increased. In the lower part of the drop-down menu, the original design is that the game provides a subordinate mechanism, which means that when the user is promoted to a certain level, a new soldier avatar will appear, and the maximum number of soldiers the user can have in the entire game is 5. After completing this design, due to the overall integration of the webpage, my code in this part can't be well connected with the code of the classmates who wrote the answer mode. So I deleted the function of the number of soldiers, and the quantity is directly set to 5. For the function of the Soldier's Exhibition Hall, due to the limited number of displays, I used a toggle button instead of the original left and right switch to display the portrait of the soldier. In partB, we used flash to show the final title of each user. In partC, I implemented this function with code. Through Jquery, the image showing the user's title appears slowly from top to bottom. At the same time, when the user's title is completely displayed, the css animation adds a stamping effect from large to small.
In the report, I am mainly responsible for the changes made by our website from partB to partC based on the feedback from the classmates and the integration of the final report. For the final trade show, my task is to introduce the web page to the user and tutor who experience our web page, such as the purpose of the web page, what data is used, what is the interaction form of the data and web page, etc. .

Part C

About

Reflection

In the process of completing this project, my team members and I are very careful and responsible. Although there are still some shortcomings in this project, I think it is still successful overall. In the implementation phase of partC, we gave up a lot of the original design features. For example, first, the background map of the web page should mark the location of the history where the history occurs, as each historical question appears. Second,as the level of users increases, the number of subordinates increases. Third, the user level is not downgraded because the user answers the wrong question. We unanimously believe that we are giving up these functions since we all think that we can't make real results. However, when I went to visit and experience other people's webpages in the final trade show, I found that some groups had done very well. I am very impressed with two groups. The first group is a typing game. Their web pages are not only very cool, but also very novel. The webpage provides the user with the content of typing, and the speed of the music is adjusted according to the speed of the user's typing. The faster the user types, the faster the music is. Finally there is a ranking for each user. The second group is a game similar to pockman, which uses the map data to display Australian animals corresponding to different street scenes according to the user's location, and the user can click on the animal to load it into the backpack. These two games give me a deeper understanding of interactivity.

At the beginning of our design, our website game is ready to adopt the question-answer mode. But when I saw the presentations of other groups on the trade show, I found that our thinking was too limited. The game is a good way to let the web page interact with the user. Similarly, there are many modes of the game. The two games I mentioned just may not be as difficult, but the two groups of students are good at innovation and pay more attention to how to make the data display in a novel way to attract users. After asking them how to rank, I found out that we didn't understand how the database interact with the front-end . The school provides us with a corresponding database platform and some interfaces. We did not make reasonable use of it, and lacked communication with teachers and classmates in the process of completing the project, and did not get targeted help. In the process of completing the final project, our team leader focused on design, one classmate is responsible for PHP, and the remaining two students are responsible for the code. However, we found that in the process of website integration, we always have to constantly compress the code we wrote and send it to other students. Then other student's laptop may have incompatibility problems with this part of the code. Once there are minor bugs that need to be modified, they must correct them and send them to other team members. During the trade show and other people's communication, I found that some groups used github to avoid the problems mentioned above. I hope that I can learn github and use it myself in future Deco courses.I am very grateful to this course for learning a lot. Last year 7140 we focused on the aesthetic design of the web page and the use of basic html, css. This year's 7180 let me know that a good web page is attractive enough both aesthetically and functionally. At the same time, each website is inseparable from the data. How to use PHP to obtain data and update the data is the focus of my future study.
